## Goal

Build a custom brass 1-inlet / 3-outlet manifold using standard threaded fittings. No soldering or welding. Outdoor use. GEKA couplings on all outlets. True 1" (DN25) bore throughout — no restrictions.

## Construction Plan

The rail is built from two AG/AG/AG brass tees chained inline vertically. One tee provides a front-facing outlet; the other provides the intake. The two remaining outlets are handled by elbows at the top and bottom of the chain, turning sideways. A master ball valve between the two tees serves as a shutoff for the upper section (outlets 1 and 3). Four IG/IG flat-sealing unions provide free rotation before tightening so all ports can be aligned.

```
   [Elbow 1]     → Ball valve → GEKA adapter → outlet 3 (side)
        |
    [Union 1]    ← alignment
        |
    [Tee 1]      → Ball valve → GEKA adapter → outlet 1 (front)
        |
    [Union 2]    ← alignment
        |
    [Ball valve] ← master shutoff (AG/AG)
        |
    [Union 3]    ← alignment
        |
    [Tee 2]      → 20 cm intake extension → intake
        |
    [Union 4]    ← alignment
        |
   [Elbow 2]     → Ball valve → GEKA adapter → outlet 2 (side)
```

All unions are IG/IG (female). Tees and elbows are AG/AG/AG and AG/AG (male) respectively. The three outlet ball valves are IG/IG (female) — the outlet tee branch (AG) and GEKA adapter (AG) each screw directly in. The master ball valve is AG/AG (male) — its top port screws directly into Union 2's bottom IG port and its bottom port screws directly into Union 3's top IG port. No double nipples needed. Union 1 and Union 4 align the elbows; Union 2 and Union 3 align the tees around the master ball valve. Flow enters at Tee 2 and moves both upward (through the master valve to outlets 1 and 3) and downward (to outlet 2, which is always on when the intake is pressurised — controlled by its own branch ball valve). All fixed threaded joints are sealed with hemp/paste or sealing cord.

### Key Design Rules

- Use **BSP / G-thread (parallel)** fittings throughout — not tapered R/NPT.
- Use **flat-sealing unions** (flachdichtend, Überwurfmutter) at every junction that needs alignment freedom — elbows need them to turn sideways, tees need them to aim branch ports, and the master ball valve needs them on both sides so both tees stay independently alignable.
- Unions = **IG/IG** (female both ends). Tees and elbows = **AG/AG** (male). Outlet ball valves = **IG/IG** (female). **Master ball valve = AG/AG** (male) — connects directly between two IG/IG unions without needing double nipples.
- Use **sealing cord or hemp + paste** for adjustable threaded joints; PTFE tape alone is less forgiving for alignment.
- Mount the finished rail rigidly with pipe clamps so hose torque is not carried by the threaded joints.
- Do **not** use conical-sealing (konisch) unions — they lock rotation at final thread depth.

### Mounting the Rail

Use **3 pipe clamps** (Rohrschellen) to fix the finished manifold rigidly to the wall. Placement:

1. **Tee 1 body** — clamp the hexagonal/flat centre section of the upper tee. This secures the top half of the chain.
2. **Master ball valve body** — clamp the valve body itself. The mid-point clamp carries most of the hose torque.
3. **Tee 2 body** — clamp the hexagonal centre section of the lower tee. This secures the intake and bottom elbow.

The tee bodies are hexagonal (not round), but standard 1" (DN25 / 33 mm) pipe clamps with **rubber inserts** (Rohrschelle mit Gummi) conform to the hex profile when tightened. Position the clamps so they don't obstruct the union nuts (which must remain freely rotatable during assembly). After alignment and final tightening of all joints, tighten the clamps fully.

Use **stainless steel clamps** (Edelstahl VA / V4A) — do not use plain galvanised steel outdoors (rusts). Each clamp mounts with an **M8×80 mm stainless steel hanger bolt** (Stockschraube) into a wall anchor. The M8 thread in the clamp nut accepts the hanger bolt directly; no separate nut is needed.

For wall mounting:
- If mounting to brick/concrete: drill 8 mm hole, use **8×50 mm wall plugs** (e.g. Fischer UX 8×50).
- If mounting to a wooden post/frame: use **stainless wood screws** 5×60 mm.

Do **not** clamp on the unions (they rotate) or on the elbows/ball valves (their profile is too irregular to clamp securely).

### Notes on unions (Part 2)

All four unions are identical — IG×IG flat-sealing. Their role:
- **Union 1**: between Elbow 1 (top) and Tee 1 — aligns both.
- **Union 2**: between Tee 1 and the master ball valve — aligns Tee 1. Master ball valve AG screws directly into bottom IG port.
- **Union 3**: between the master ball valve and Tee 2 — aligns Tee 2. Master ball valve AG screws directly into top IG port.
- **Union 4**: between Tee 2 and Elbow 2 (bottom) — aligns both.

Avoid plain IG/IG sleeves without a union nut — they do not provide alignment freedom.

### If union bore is uncertain

The union must not restrict flow below ~DN20 (20 mm internal diameter = 314 mm²). A true G1" union should pass DN25 (25 mm, 491 mm²). Always verify the stated internal bore before ordering — "1 inch" on the label does not guarantee a full-bore passage.
