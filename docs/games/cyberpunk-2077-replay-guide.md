## 3. Key Findings

### 3.1 Mod foundations — install these first

A stable mod setup requires a framework layer before adding any content mods [S-NLM, S3, S7]:

- **Cyber Engine Tweaks (CET)** — mandatory base for most advanced mods; also provides a debug console and TAA toggling.
- **Red4Ext, Redscript, ArchiveXL, TweakXL, Codeware** — script and asset extension layers.
- **Mod Settings / Native Settings UI** — in-game mod configuration without touching `.ini` files.
- **ConflictChecker** — the single most important tool for load-order management in Vortex or manual installs [S-NLM].
- **Vortex or Mod Organizer 2** — mod manager; either works, personal preference.

> **REDmod caveat:** Experienced modders advise treating REDmod-specific mods with caution — perceived stability issues in 100+ hour save files. Community frameworks (CET, ArchiveXL) are more reliable long-term [S-NLM].

### 3.3 Top immersion and story mods (post-2.0 compatible)

**World reactivity and NPC interaction:**

- **Dark Future** [S4, S-NLM] — survival needs (hunger, thirst, sleep, relax). Replaces the older "Survival System" mod. Default settings are punishing — tune them immediately via Mod Settings UI.
- **They Will Remember** [S5, S-NLM] — gang faction memory/revenge system; gangs target you for violence on their turf. Post-2.1 compatible.
- **Night City Alive** [S6] — more gang activity and ambient world events.
- **Immersive Gigs** [S-NLM] — you must actively contact fixers to request work; removes automatic gig pings.
- **Night City Interactions** [S-NLM] — enables sitting at bars and drinking from a glass (not standing-chug animations).
- **Immersive Bartenders / Food Vendors / Rippers** (by deceptious) [S-NLM] — animated NPC interactions. Require separate Dogtown add-ons for Phantom Liberty areas.

**HUD and navigation:**

- **Limited HUD (LHUD)** [S-NLM, S2] — contextual HUD hiding (minimap, quest markers, health bars via hotkey). Consistently described as "peak immersion" by veteran players.
- **In-World Navigation** [S2] — holographic navigation arrows on roads; removes minimap dependency.

**Character and roleplay depth:**

- **Wannabe Edgerunner** [S12] — adds a cyberpsychosis/humanity system tied to cyberware installation. Creates mechanical stakes for build choices.
- **Equipment-EX** [S3] — unlimited outfit slots; separates fashion from armour stats.
- **No More Duplicate NPCs** [S7] — randomises NPC clothing; reduces the "clone army" effect.
- **Cutscene Weapon Swapper** [S3] — V holds your actual equipped weapon in cutscenes instead of a generic prop.
- **Romance Enhanced / Romance Messages Extended** [S6] — expanded partner dialogue and daily-routine calls.

**Visual (secondary but recommended):**

- **Nova LUT** ("Pure" or "Classic" variants) [S-NLM, S1] — removes the stock warm-yellow tint. Zero performance cost. Controversial among purists — see Section 5.
- **Realistic Map** [S-NLM] — replaces the stylised 3D map with a high-res 2D render; easier indoor navigation.

### 3.5 Phantom Liberty — when to start it

Strong community consensus favours **starting Phantom Liberty late, not early** [S13, S14, S15]:

1. **Unlock condition:** PL becomes available after completing the Voodoo Boys' "Transmission" quest in Act 2.
2. **Optimal narrative approach:** Delay the Voodoo Boys questline until you have exhausted the main story options and most side content. Then trigger PL as V's last-resort gamble — right before the "Nocturne Op55N1" point-of-no-return.
3. **Why late works:** This frames Phantom Liberty as an extended Act 3 replacement rather than a gear-farm detour. The emotional weight of the sacrifice is maximised.
4. **Side quest synergy:** Completing base-game character arcs (Panam, Judy, River, Kerry) before entering Dogtown unlocks additional dialogue and cameos inside PL missions.
5. **Counter-argument:** Starting early gives access to the Relic perk tree and Dogtown gear sooner, which some players prefer for mechanical reasons (see Section 5).

### 3.6 Replay freshness strategies (no mods required)

- **Strict roleplay persona:** Pick one before you start. Community favourites: Judge Dredd enforcer (non-lethal only on civilians, lethal on criminals), Pacifist AI (zero kills), Dexter DeShawn serial killer (every mark is a contract), Corpo elitist (only Corpo lifepath dialogue, no gang alliances), Traditionalist Nomad (no fast travel, obey traffic, sleep schedule).
- **Radically different build archetype:** If you played Netrunner before, commit to Solo Berserker or Stealth Ninja. The perks, cyberware, and rhythm of combat feel like different games.
- **Realistic daily routines:** Sleep at the apartment between missions, use only in-world navigation, obey traffic laws, only accept gigs at night. Costs almost nothing to impose, adds enormous texture.
- **"Master save" trick:** Create a manual save immediately after The Heist with 0 spent attribute/perk points and store it externally. Use it as a replay starting point for future runs without replaying the prologue.
- **Flip your dialogue style:** If you defaulted to empathetic/polite in the first run, commit to the most abrasive or mercenary option in every conversation.
- **Allow memory to fade:** The community broadly agrees that 2+ years between playthroughs lets enough story detail fade to feel genuinely fresh again.

## 5. Conflicts & Open Questions

- **Conflict — LUT visual mods:** [S1, S2] and [S-NLM] strongly recommend Nova LUT as an essential immersion upgrade. A vocal minority counters that these mods strip out the developers' intentional neon-amber aesthetic and produce a "washed-out London-weather look" [S-NLM gap query 1]. A user identifying as a professional colorist publicly defends the mods; CDPR's own artistic rationale is undocumented. Neither side is clearly more authoritative. **Recommended:** try Nova LUT Classic for one session and revert if it feels wrong.

- **Conflict — Phantom Liberty timing:** [S10, S11, S13, S14] favour starting late (narrative weight); [S15] and some community voices favour starting early (Relic tree access, Dogtown gear). Both are defensible. The late-start is the majority view and is recommended here, but early-start is a valid choice for players who prioritise mechanical progression over story cohesion.

- **Conflict — REDmod stability:** [S-NLM] reports experienced modders avoiding REDmod-specific mods for long saves. No primary data quantifies the failure rate. This claim is plausible but unverified beyond anecdote.

- **Unresolved — Patch 2.3/2.31 mod compatibility:** Sources confirm patch 2.3 introduced vehicle customisation changes that invalidated some mod lists [S-NLM gap query 2]. The full scope of compatibility breakage for the specific mods recommended in this report is not verified. Cross-check every mod's Nexus Mods page for patch 2.3 compatibility before installing.

- **Unresolved — Enemies of Night City new version:** The community mentions a new test version is in approval [S-NLM gap query 6]. Status unclear as of report date.

## 7. Recommended Next Steps

1. **Verify mod compatibility against current patch before installing.** Patch 2.3/2.31 broke some modlists. Check the Nexus Mods page for each recommended mod (filter by "last updated" and read the posts tab for patch compatibility notes).

2. **Start with a collection, not a manual build.** Install Welcome to Night City or 3077: Ultimate Vanilla+ first, play for 2–3 hours, then add individual mods (Dark Future, They Will Remember, LHUD, Wannabe Edgerunner) one at a time and test after each addition.

3. **Set your roleplay rules before you launch.** Write down your persona constraints (build archetype, dialogue stance, travel rules, sleep schedule) and keep them visible. The community consensus is that self-imposed rules are the single biggest driver of replayability — more than any mod.

4. **Delay the Voodoo Boys questline.** This is the trigger for Phantom Liberty. Don't touch "Transmission" until you have completed Panam's, Judy's, River's, and Kerry's storylines and are ready for the game's final act.

5. **Follow up on Patch 2.3 notes specifically.** The full scope of what changed in patch 2.3/2.31a is missing from the research sources. Read the official CDPR patch notes before finalising your modlist.

6. **Do not install Wannabe Edgerunner without reading the configuration options first.** The default settings apply cyberpsychosis penalties aggressively. If you want it as flavour rather than a hardcore survival system, tune the thresholds in Mod Settings before your first session.

7. **Check the Welcome to Night City Discord for the latest compatible modlist.** The WtNC collection is actively maintained and the Discord is the fastest way to get the current recommended version — faster than any static guide.
