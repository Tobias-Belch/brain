## 3. Cassette / Blade Costs & Replacement Frequency

### Braun Series 3 3040s

- **Replacement unit:** Braun 32b (black) or 32s (silver) cassette — foil + inner blades in one unit
- **German price:** ~15–20 EUR (Amazon.de, idealo.de; April 2026)
- **Official replacement interval:** Every 18 months (Braun guidance)
- **Practical interval:** 12–18 months depending on usage frequency and hair coarseness
- **What the cassette includes:** All cutting elements — foil screens and inner blades — replaced as a single unit

### Braun Series 5 51-B1000s

- **Important model note:** The current Series 5 (51/52-series bodies, e.g. 51-B1000s) uses **different cassettes** than the older Series 5 (50-series, 5090cc). Older 52s/52b cassettes are not compatible.
- **Replacement unit:** Braun 53b (standard) or 54b (with precision trimmer) cassette
- **German price:** ~20–25 EUR (Amazon.de, idealo.de; April 2026)
- **Official replacement interval:** Every 18 months (Braun guidance)
- **Practical interval:** 12–18 months
- **What the cassette includes:** All foil and blade elements as a single unit. The 51-B1000s (solo model) does not come with a cleaning station — no alcohol-cartridge ongoing cost for this variant

### Panasonic Arc 5 ES-LV67

- **Replacement units:** Foil and inner blades are **sold separately** (unlike Braun's all-in-one cassette)
  - Foil frame: **ES9040** (current generation; backward-compatible with Gen 3 Arc 5 bodies including ES-LV67)
  - Inner blades: **WES9170**
- **German prices (April 2026):**
  - ES9040 foil set: ~40–55 EUR
  - WES9170 inner blades: ~25–35 EUR
  - **Combined per replacement cycle: ~65–90 EUR**
- **Official replacement intervals:** Foil every 12 months, blades every 24 months (Panasonic guidance)
- **Practical interval:** Replace both together every ~18 months — aligns replacement cycles and reduces maintenance overhead
- **Cost implication:** Panasonic's split foil/blade structure makes replacement significantly more expensive than either Braun model on a per-cycle basis

## 4. Dry vs. Wet Shaving

"Wet shaving" with an electric foil shaver means operating it under running water or with shaving cream / gel applied. "Dry shaving" means using it on dry skin with no water or product.

### Braun Series 3 3040s

- **Wet/dry capable:** Yes — the 3040s is a cordless-wet/dry model. It can be used in the shower or with shaving foam/gel.
- **Important variant note:** Braun Series 3 cc variants (3050cc, 3070cc, 3090cc) are dry-only during corded operation and generally do not support foam/gel use. The 3040s (cordless only) is the wet/dry version.
- **Wet use benefit:** Moderately helpful for sensitive skin; softens stubble and reduces friction. Not as impactful as on the Panasonic.

### Braun Series 5 51-B1000s

- **Wet/dry capable:** Yes — all current Series 5 (51/52-series) models are wet/dry and cordless-only. The 51-B1000s supports wet use including shaving foam and gel.
- **Wet use benefit:** Useful for sensitive skin. Braun's already-comfortable dry performance means the wet gain is less transformative than on the Arc 5.

### Panasonic Arc 5 ES-LV67

- **Wet/dry capable:** Yes — the ES-LV67 is fully waterproof and designed for wet use.
- **Wet use benefit:** Strongly recommended for sensitive skin. The Arc 5's 14,000 CPM linear motor is more aggressive than Braun's sonic approach and can cause dry-skin irritation. A thin layer of shaving gel substantially bridges the comfort gap noted in the source report.
- **Wet use recommendation:** If skin comfort is a concern, use the Arc 5 wet before concluding it irritates. The dry vs. wet difference is larger on this model than on either Braun.

| Braun Series 5 51-B1000s | Yes (all current 51/52-series) | Moderate — already comfortable dry |

## 5. Cleaning Process

### Braun Series 3 3040s

- **Method:** Manual only (the 3040s is a solo model with no cleaning station option)
- **Process:**
  1. Tap water rinse — hold cassette under running warm water
  2. Add a small drop of liquid soap; run the shaver briefly under water with soap
  3. Rinse thoroughly; tap dry; air dry before storing
  4. Use the included small brush only on the inner blade area, not on the foil screens (brushing foil screens damages them)
- **Difficulty:** Easy — 1–2 minutes. The 32b/32s cassette design is straightforward to rinse.
- **After wet shave:** Must use soap in addition to water rinse (foam/gel residue). Process remains fast.
- **Frequency:** After every use (recommended); at minimum every 2–3 uses.

### Braun Series 5 51-B1000s

- **Method:** Manual (the 51-B1000s solo model has no cleaning station; cc variants — 52-B7200cc, 52-N7650cc — include alcohol-based Braun Clean & Renew stations)
- **Process (manual):**
  1. Tap water rinse under warm water
  2. Add liquid soap; run briefly; rinse thoroughly
  3. Tap dry; air dry
  4. Use included brush for inner debris — same caution: do not brush foil screens
- **Difficulty:** Moderate — the 53b/54b cassette geometry is more enclosed than the Series 3 cassette; harder to fully flush debris manually. Users who prefer a very thorough clean benefit more from a cleaning station on this model.
- **After wet shave:** Same as Series 3 — soap + water rinse. No significant added difficulty.
- **Frequency:** After every use recommended.

### Panasonic Arc 5 ES-LV67

- **Method:** Manual only — the ES-LV67 does not support a cleaning station (no charging/contact pins on the body designed for a station dock)
- **Process:**
  1. Open the foil frame release — the outer foil frame pops off with one press
  2. Hold inner blades and foil frame under warm running water separately
  3. Apply a drop of liquid soap; gently rub with fingers; rinse
  4. Air dry separately before reassembling
  5. **Sonic cleaning mode (recommended):** Hold the power button for 2 seconds while the shaver is on — triggers a 20-second high-speed blade oscillation that shakes out debris from the blade assembly. Do this before or after rinsing for deeper cleaning.
- **Difficulty:** Easy — faster to disassemble and reassemble than the Braun cassette designs. Sonic mode adds depth without extra time.
- **After wet shave:** Liquid soap + rinse + Sonic mode. Slightly more residue to clear than dry shave, but still fast.
- **Frequency:** After every use recommended, particularly for wet shave.

### Summary

| Model | Manual method | Difficulty | Station available | After wet shave |

| Braun Series 3 3040s | Water + soap rinse; brush inner area | Easy | No (3040s is solo) | Same process; must use soap |
| Braun Series 5 51-B1000s | Water + soap rinse; brush inner area | Moderate — cassette harder to flush | cc variants only (alcohol-based) | Same process; soap required |

## 6. 96M Cassette Compatibility

The **Braun 96M cassette** was introduced for the **Braun Series 9 Pro+** lineup (93xx, 94xx, 95xx, 96xx bodies). It features Ultra Thin Precision Blades that close Braun's historical closeness gap with Panasonic.

**Compatibility with Series 3 and Series 5: No.**

The 96M cassette is physically and mechanically incompatible with both Series 3 and Series 5 bodies. These are completely separate cassette families:

| Series | Cassette family | 96M fits? |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| Series 3 (3040s) | 32b / 32s | No |
| Series 5 (51-B1000s) | 53b / 54b | No |
| Series 9 Pro+ (9515s) | 92b / 92s / 94b / 96M | N/A (native) |

There is no upgrade path from the 96M technology down to Series 3 or Series 5. If the 96M's improved closeness is a priority, the only option is to purchase a Series 9-compatible body.

## 7. Non-Electric & Partially Electric Alternatives

For completeness, the main non-electric alternatives are summarized here as a comparison class against the three foil shavers. All non-electric methods are wet-shave only — they require water and typically shaving cream or gel.

### Cartridge razors (e.g. Gillette Fusion5, Wilkinson Hydro)

- **Handle cost (one-time):** ~10–25 EUR
- **Cartridge cost:** ~3–6 EUR per cartridge; typical use 1–2 weeks per cartridge
- **Annual blade cost (daily shaver):** ~75–150 EUR/year
- **Closeness:** Very good — direct-blade contact achieves very close results on fresh stubble
- **Skin comfort:** Moderate — multi-blade cartridges can cause irritation and ingrown hairs, particularly on sensitive skin or coarse/curly hair
- **Speed per shave:** ~5–8 minutes (requires warm water prep and lather)
- **Flat-lying/neck hair:** Good — manual control and varied angles
- **Ongoing complexity:** Moderate — requires shaving cream/gel every use; no dry option

### Safety razor (double-edge / DE razor)

- **Handle cost (one-time):** ~35–70 EUR (e.g. Merkur 34C, Edwin Jagger DE89)
- **Blade cost:** ~0.20–0.50 EUR per blade; replace every 3–7 shaves
- **Annual blade cost (daily shaver):** ~10–30 EUR/year
- **Closeness:** Excellent — single, extremely sharp blade; matches or exceeds foil shavers on short stubble
- **Skin comfort:** Variable — high learning curve; irritation and nicks common in the first weeks. Long-term users report very low irritation. Not recommended for coarse/curly hair due to ingrown hair risk.
- **Speed per shave:** ~10–15 minutes (full wet prep, multi-pass technique required)
- **Flat-lying/neck hair:** Good with technique; requires proper angle control
- **Ongoing complexity:** Low cost; high skill investment; no dry option

### Straight razor (cut-throat razor)

- **Handle/blade cost (one-time):** ~60–200 EUR for a quality razor; ~30–60 EUR for a leather strop
- **Blade cost:** Essentially zero ongoing — blade is honed and stropped, not replaced
- **Annual ongoing cost:** Near zero (strop wear, occasional honing service ~15–30 EUR every 1–2 years)
- **Closeness:** Maximum — the reference standard for closeness
- **Skin comfort:** High risk during learning curve (months); excellent long-term for skilled users
- **Speed per shave:** ~15–25 minutes
- **Flat-lying/neck hair:** Excellent with technique
- **Ongoing complexity:** Very high skill requirement; no dry option; not suited for occasional shavers

### Disposable razors

- **Cost:** ~0.30–1.50 EUR per razor; typically 1–5 uses per razor
- **Annual cost (daily shaver):** ~50–100 EUR/year
- **Closeness:** Moderate — inferior to premium cartridge or DE options
- **Skin comfort:** Often poor — cheap blades drag and irritate
- **Speed:** Similar to cartridge razor
- **Use case:** Travel convenience only; not a quality daily shaving solution

### Reading the scores

The Braun Series 3 scores highest on the 5-year relative rating by a wide margin — it delivers 67/100 performance for approximately 110 EUR over five years. The Braun Series 5 provides a moderate step up in absolute performance (73/100) at a significantly higher 5-year cost, yielding a lower relative score. The Panasonic Arc 5 delivers the best absolute performance (80/100) but at nearly 3.5× the 5-year cost of the Braun Series 3, producing the lowest relative score by a large margin.

**Contrast with the source report's original relative score (Abs/Purchase price):**

| Model | Rel. Score (original: Abs/Price) | 5-yr Rel. Score (Abs/5yr cost) |
|-------|----------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Braun Series 3 3040s | 128.8 | 60.9 |

The Arc 5's relative attractiveness drops most sharply when ownership cost replaces purchase price — its expensive foil+blade replacement structure more than doubles the total cost of ownership vs. either Braun model. The ranking order is unchanged, but the magnitude of the gap increases substantially.

### Interpretation

- **If budget over 5 years is a primary constraint:** The Braun Series 3 is the dominant choice. Its total 5-year cost (~110 EUR) is lower than the Arc 5's purchase price alone.
- **If absolute closeness justifies a premium:** The Arc 5 remains the best-performing option in this tier, but buyers should be aware that the 5-year cost (~384 EUR) approaches the purchase price of a Braun Series 9 Pro+ (~270 EUR) — which scores 89/100 absolute grade. For daily shavers with a 5-year horizon, the Series 9 Pro+ total cost (~270 + ~200 in 96M cassettes ≈ ~470 EUR) is only marginally more expensive than the Arc 5, at substantially higher performance.
- **Safety razor as an alternative:** A quality DE safety razor setup costs ~85–185 EUR over 5 years (handle + 5 years of blades) — cheaper than the Braun Series 3 — while delivering excellent closeness. The trade-off is exclusively time and technique: wet prep, slower shave, and a learning curve. For users willing to make that trade, it is the highest-value shaving method in absolute terms.
