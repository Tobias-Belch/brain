# System Evaluation: 1" GEKA-Compatible Piping Systems

## Scoring Criteria
- **Cost**: Total BOM cost (lower = better)
- **Assembly difficulty**: Tools needed, skill level
- **Durability / longevity**: Outdoor/wet conditions
- **Flow/pressure**: Bore size and fitting losses
- **Reversibility**: Can it be disassembled and modified?

---

## System A: Galvanized Steel (Temperguss verzinkt + Gewinderohr)

**BOM Total: ~69,75 €**

### Cost
Cheapest option by ~21 €. Standard threaded fittings at 2,48–9 € each. 300mm nipple: 4,46 €. GEKA faucets dominate cost at 43,88 € (shared across all systems).

### Assembly Difficulty: Medium
- Requires PTFE tape (cheap) and a pipe wrench (most people own one or can borrow)
- Threaded joints: straightforward but requires effort for 1" wrench size
- Pipe wrench needed for 33,7mm OD pipe — standard hardware
- Wall passage: cut 300mm galvanized nipple to exact 270mm with hacksaw or angle grinder

### Durability
- **Indoor/dry**: Excellent; 25+ year lifespan
- **Outdoor/damp**: Galvanized zinc coating provides moderate corrosion resistance
- **Buried or in wall**: Acceptable but galvanized can rust over 10–20 years in damp concrete
- **Risk**: Thread corrosion if joints get wet repeatedly; use PTFE + thread sealant paste

### Flow/Pressure
- Full bore 1" (DN25): good flow
- Temperguss elbows have moderate turbulence (radius not as smooth as brass)
- No appreciable pressure drop difference vs. other systems at garden pump pressures

### Reversibility
- Fully reversible (unscrew with wrench)
- Can swap fittings if layout changes

### Score: ★★★★☆
Best for: budget-conscious install, DIY, indoor or covered outdoor location.

---

## System B: PE 32mm + PP Compression Fittings

**BOM Total: ~90,83 €**

### Cost
~21 € more than System A. Higher PE pipe and fitting costs offset by no corrosion risk. 1m PE pipe segment for wall passage: 8,60 €.

### Assembly Difficulty: Easy
- No tools required beyond hand-tightening compression nuts
- PP Klemmverbinder: push pipe in, tighten nut — takes <30 seconds per joint
- PE pipe is flexible: easier to maneuver through wall bore
- Must ensure PE pipe end is cut square (use pipe cutter ~10 €, or scissors-type PVC cutter)

### Durability
- **Outdoor/buried**: Excellent — PE100 is UV-stable (if black) and corrosion-free
- **In wall**: Best option for buried or wall-embedded segment; no rust ever
- **Expected lifespan**: 50+ years (PE pipe warranty typically 25–50 years)
- Compression fittings: rated PN16 DVGW; reliable at garden pump pressures

### Flow/Pressure
- 32mm OD, ~26mm ID (3mm wall) — slightly larger bore than 1" galvanized pipe (~27mm ID)
- Smooth bore, no scale build-up
- Slightly better flow characteristics than threaded steel

### Reversibility
- Compression fittings can be un-tightened and repositioned
- PE pipe can be recut
- Good flexibility for future modifications

### Score: ★★★★★
Best for: permanent outdoor or buried install, tool-light assembly, best long-term durability.

---

## System C: Brass / Stainless Steel Threaded

**BOM Total: ~90,78 €**

### Cost
Similar to System B (~21 € more than System A). Brass elbows ~9,59 €/pc vs. 2,48–4,47 € for galvanized. Premium materials justified by durability and food safety.

### Assembly Difficulty: Medium
- Same as System A: PTFE tape + pipe wrench
- Brass fittings easier to hand-start (softer than iron)
- Stainless nipple cut with hacksaw (harder than galvanized)

### Durability
- **Outdoor/damp**: Excellent — brass CW617N and stainless 304/316 do not rust
- **In wall**: Best of threaded systems for wall embedding; no corrosion in concrete
- **Expected lifespan**: 50+ years
- Food-safe; DIN 50930-6 (Trinkwasserverordnung) compliant

### Flow/Pressure
- Same 1" (27mm ID) threaded bore as System A
- Brass has smoother internal finish than galvanized iron

### Reversibility
- Fully reversible (threaded joints)
- Can be disassembled with wrench

### Score: ★★★★☆
Best for: quality-first install with threaded convenience, no corrosion, food-safe.

---

## ❌ Excluded: Copper 28mm Solder/Press-fit

- Copper solder elbows 1" (28mm OD) cost 15–30 €/pc on Amazon.de
- Requires torch soldering or expensive press-fit tool (>100 €)
- Total BOM would exceed 150 €+
- **Verdict: Not justified for garden watering application. Eliminated.**

## Recommendation

**Primary recommendation: System A (Galvanized Steel)** if the pipe run is above ground, covered, or in a dry location. Best value, straightforward assembly.

**If buried, in wall, or long-term concern**: System B (PE + PP) for its corrosion immunity and tool-free assembly. The ~21 € premium is worth it for a permanent installation.

**System C** is a good middle ground if you prefer traditional threaded plumbing with zero corrosion risk, but offers no significant advantage over System B at the same price point.
